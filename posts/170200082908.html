Founder of the “first online television network” Josh Harris gives a tour of the Pseudo.com offices in soho, nyc. Pseudo was a forerunner in the streaming/podcasting content space, but went bust during the dotcom bubble. 

In 2008, Harris published an open letter on boingboing claiming that Pseudo had been a “long form piece of conceptual art”, hyped by a later-discredited New York Times reporter named Jayson Blair. “Pseudo burned over $25 million in private and institutional capital over a span of seven years. Pseudo was a fake company.”
